The GNI and HPI in 2016

This visualization was made get a view on the possible link between rich countries and their happiness and how happy they leave the planet.

To show how "rich" a country is the first visualization shows a map of the world colored to show the GNI or Gross National Income per capita at purchasing power parity in USD in 2016. The darker the country, the "richer" that country is. To show the happiness of people and how "happy" a country leaves the earth, the scatterplot underneath the map shows the HPI or Happy Planet Index. The HPI is made up out of a couple of things which include; ecological footprint, life expectancy and equality of outcome within a country.

Because the world has a lot of countries and HPI has different factors you can click on a county. After clicking a country in one of the visualizations it gets a green border in both of the visualizations. If you don't want to see that country highlighted anymore, you can just click it again. To see the different factors of the HPI there are buttons to change what variable you see on which axis of the scatterplot.

To get the exact data of one of the variables just keep your mouse on one of the countries or datapoints, within a few seconds the name of the country and the exact data will appear.
